Numerical Example (change . into , if needed)
Input coordinates of a terrain-point are given with respect to the Bessel 1841 ellipsoid: (of the Dutch 'Rijksdriehoeksmeting')
latitude phi1 = 52.2 (52 degr: 12' north); longitude lam1 = 6.9 (6 degr: 54' east);
height1 above the Bessel ellipsoid = 32.46 m; This is a point near Enschede, The Netherlands

The approximate datum transformation parameters from the Dutch "Amersfoort" datum to the WGS84 datum are as follows:
Shifts: tx = 565, ty = 50, tz = 466 (m); the vector connecting the origin of the tri-axial coordinate frame of WGS84 to the origin of the Bessel tri-axial coordinate frame, all in terms of components wrt the WGS84 axes.
Rotations: Rx = 1985e-9, Ry = -1744e-9, Rz = 9059e-9 (radians); the (linearized) rotations needed to bring the Bessel axes parallel to the WGS84 axes.
Scale change: s = 4077e-9 (m); the difference in unit of distance to account for scale difference in the 2 geodetic sytems involved.
As output ellipsoid the WGS84, the GPS defined global datum, is selected. Not selecting an output-ellipsoid produces an error message.

The output coordinates of the terrain-point with respect to the WGS84 ellipsoid will be calculated as:
latitude phi2 = 52.199232; longitude lam2 = 6.900123;
height2 above the WGS84 ellipsoid = 75.745 m;

Conclusion: the given terrain point lies about 85 m closer to the WGS84-equator than to the Bessel equator
and it is 43.285 m higher above the WGS84 surface compared to the Bessel surface.
The latter surface corresponds closely to the NAP 0-level, the Dutch vertical datum
